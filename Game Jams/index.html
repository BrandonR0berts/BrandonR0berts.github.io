Rise Of The Spitfire

As an end to the first year, my university organised a week long game jam in conjunction with the Stoke BID, putting forward seven themes in total for the jam.

Throughout the jam I worked with two other students, first coming up with game ideas and designs for the theme we were given; 'Spitfire'. Overall we came up with the idea of making a twin stick shooter, inspired by arcade games of the same genre such as 1942, and more recent games such as Cuphead's flying level. Building on this idea I worked as the solo programmer to create an endless level in which enemy planes spawn in and attack the player, who is a playing as a Spitfire plane. The other two students created the art for the project, giving it a pixelated, and somewhat cartoony feel to it. After submitting the game to the jam we waited a while during the game was made avaliable to the public in Stoke City Centre, as part of a push to get people back into city centres. During the awards ceremony it was revealed that we had won our category as well as overall, with the main feedback given that people wanted more levels, and that it felt like the 'most complete game in the contest overall.'

Zombie Escape

For my first 48 hour game jam I worked in a small team to create a game based around the theme of 'Running Out Of Space.'

For this game jam submission I worked with one other programmer, working to the theme of running out of space. As we both had had some prior experience working using SDL2 in C++, we decided to use SDL as the base renderer of the project. Building from this we decided to go with a zombie style game where you need to build a pile of body parts in order to reach an escape helicopter. This would force the player to shoot the zombies when they are closer to the centre of the level, making the player 'run out of space.' As a first game jam project the experience was very good, extremely stressful towards the end, but I learnt a lot from the experience that I have applied to more recent projects.
